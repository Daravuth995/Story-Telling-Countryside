A Countryside Experience

I always thought the countryside/ˈkʌn.tri.saɪd/Land outside cities and towns was boring and that I could never live anywhere except the city. My parents wanted me to spend a week with my uncle on his small farm. At first, I wasn't excited. I imagined/ɪˈmædʒ.ɪnd/Formed a picture in your mind waking up early, doing chores/tʃɔːrz/Small jobs around the house or farm, and having nothing fun to do. I also thought I would miss my phone and the internet.

Pronunciation Practice: Rising & Falling Intonation

In English, the way your voice rises or falls can completely change how natural and clear you sound. This exercise will help you practice using intonation to speak more fluidly and confidently.

↘️ Part 1: Falling Intonation (Statement, Command, or WH-Question)

These examples come from the story and reflect confident, complete thoughts.

  • I had an experience that changed how I see country life. (Falling – Statement)
  • I always thought the countryside was boring. (Falling – Statement)
  • We had a picnic under a big tree. (Falling – Statement)
  • Now I appreciate simple moments. (Falling – Statement)

↗️ Part 2: Rising Intonation (Yes/No Question or Uncertainty)

These questions reflect how you might ask about details from the story.

  • Did you enjoy your time on the farm? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
  • What did you do in the morning? (Rising – WH-Question)
  • Have you ever spent time in the countryside? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
  • Did you think it would be boring? (Rising – Yes/No Question)
Helpful Tip:

• ↘️ Falling Intonation = shows confidence, finality, or a complete idea.

• ↗️ Rising Intonation = shows a question, uncertainty, or a request for confirmation.

• Practicing intonation helps you sound more fluent and natural in conversation.
